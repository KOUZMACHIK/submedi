Submission Guidelines

Authors are invited to submit English-language research and application papers representing original, previously unpublished work. Duplicate submissions are not allowed.

Paper Types

Full Papers

Up to 14 pages, presenting original research.

Short Papers

Up to 8 pages, describing work in progress or preliminary results.

Submissions must conform to Springer's LNCS style (Latex style only for camera-ready papers). Papers must be submitted as a single PDF document via CMT System.

Submitted papers will be carefully evaluated based on originality, significance, technical soundness, and clarity of exposition. The program committee may accept a submission as a short paper if it reports interesting results but does not justify publication as a full paper. All accepted papers, including short papers, will be published in the Springer LNCS series.

For metrics on previous MEDI proceedings, visit: https://link.springer.com/conference/medi

Publication Requirements

  • One author of an accepted paper must register for MEDI 2026 to have the paper published.
  • Each accepted paper must be presented at the conference by one of the authors.

Special Issues

Best papers will be selected and invited for submission to the Journal of Computer Languages and other journals (to be confirmed).
